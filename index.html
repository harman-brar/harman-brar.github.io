A Little Bit About Me





Very early on, I was introduced to sports and martial arts. For a long time, these were my two great passions. At the age of thirteen, I learned of programming. Facing many initial challenges and headaches I briefly turned away from coding, thinking it was not for me. Two years later, now 15, I took another crack at programming as I had an ever-increasing fascination with the practical and theoretical applications of well-written applications. This time, the readings made a lot more sense, and I was hooked. Alongside playing competitive hockey, ultimately at the Junior level, I worked hard to maintain my grades while spending as much time as I could building programs that I felt could, in some way, improve someone's experience in the world. When the opportunity came to study at UBC, I left Junior hockey, and wholeheartedly decided to pursue a degree in Computer Science. Here, I have met people with very interesting approaches to problems, as well as learned of many problems in the world that need fixing, and am constantly working hard to develop all the skills required to form a strong base to solve these problems. Having been a research assistant, I really appreciate the thought and effort that goes into a breakthrough, and would love to one day contribute to a great product that is the solution to a real world problem.
